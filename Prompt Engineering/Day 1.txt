5 Principles of Prompting
1. Giving Direction

Describe the desired style in detail, or reference a relevant persona.

Naive prompt:

Give 10 product names for shoes that fit any shoe size.

Prompt engineering:
Prompt engineering is used to yield useful and desired results.

Refined prompt example:

Brainstorm a list of product names for a shoe that fits any shoe style, in the style of Steve Jobs.

Image models: Stable Diffusion

2. Specify Format

Define what rules to follow and the required structure of the response.

3. Provide Examples

Insert a diverse set of test cases where the task was done correctly.
(Very costly)

4. Evaluate Quality

Identify errors, rate responses, and test what drives performance.

5. Divide Labor

Split tasks into multiple steps, chained together, for complex goals.
