Lecture 5 - What value BA brings to organization

1. BA are change agents. They help org make change, as the only constant in business is change.

2. Gap analysis - where they are today and what vision they have and what steps it would take to get to that vision.

3. The BA role is extremely broad. Creating, designing, supporting, asking questions etc.
 
Lecture 6 - Exploring BA position across industries

1. BA isn't just one job title.

2. Its a capability used across many roles and teams.

3. Its about identifying problems, evaluating options, and driving solutions.

4. Product and project roles using analysis:
a. Product owners and managers align work with business goals.
b. Project managers define scope, access risks and guide solutions.
c. Scrum master facilitate clarity and remove blockers in agile teams.

5. Design, operations, and support roles:
a. UX designers research and define user centered solutions.
b. Operations managers improve workflows and efficiencies.
c. Customer support and success teams surface insights for improvement.

6. Developers and Specialized Roles Applying Analysis:
a. Developers clarify requirements and shape technical solutions.
b. Compliance and risk professionals access gaps and propose improvements.
c. Marketing and sales teams analyze performance and customer behavior.

7. A shared capability, not just a title. BA cuts across departments and industry. Anyone solving problems or helping the business adapt is practicing analysis.

8. The impact of BA skills:
a. BA drives better decisions and outcomes.
b. Its for anyone who wants to make a meaningful impact.

Lecture 7 - Building BA skills in any role

1. Growing your BA skills:
a. BA can be developed from any role.
b. Its about strengthening how you think and work, not just your title.

2. Building on the work you already do:
a. Gathering feedback, solving problems, recommending improvements - all count.
b. You're already on the right path if you are practicing these daily.

3. Improve your curiosity and questioning:
a. Great analysis starts with asking what's behind the request.
b. Strong answer uncovers better, clearer answers.

4. Bring structure to Chaos:
a. Pull inputs from stakeholders systems, and documents.
b. Create clear summaries, flows or maps to make sense of the details.

5. Break down and simplify problems:
a. Divide big, vague issues into smaller, manageable parts.
b. Clarify who's affected, where it happens and what's driving it.

6. See the bigger picture:
a. Understand how problems, processes and people are connected.
b. Zoom out to spot root causes and meaningful patterns.

7. Strengthen your communication:
a. Clear analysis earns trust and lead to action.
b. Whether its a conversation or a report, clarity matters.

8. Make an impact from where you are:
a. BA is about mindset and action, not just position.
b. Start using these skills now to see real results in your work.
