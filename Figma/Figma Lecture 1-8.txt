Lecture 7 - High and low fidelity wireframe

The difference between wireframing and high-fidelity design in Figma is mainly about level of detail, purpose, and stage in the design process.

1. Wireframing (Low / Mid Fidelity)
Purpose

Focus on structure and layout, not visuals.

What it looks like
Simple boxes and placeholders
Black/white or grayscale
No real images or branding
Basic text (lorem ipsum or labels)
Focus areas
Page layout
User flow
Placement of elements (buttons, images, forms)
Navigation structure
Goal

Answer:

“What goes where and how does the user move through the screen?”

2. High-Fidelity Design (Hi-Fi)
Purpose

Focus on final look, feel, and interaction realism

What it looks like
Real colors, fonts, spacing
Actual images/icons
Branding included
Detailed UI components
Often interactive (clickable prototype in Figma)
Focus areas
Visual design system
Typography, colors, spacing
Micro-interactions (hover, transitions)
Final user experience
Goal

Answer:

“How will the final product look and feel to the user?”

Key Difference Summary
Aspect	Wireframe	High-Fidelity
Detail	Low	High
Colors/Images	No	Yes
Purpose	Structure & flow	Final design & experience
Speed	Fast to create	Time-consuming
Stage	Early design	Late design
Feedback focus	Layout & usability	Visual & UX polish
Simple Way to Remember
Wireframe = Skeleton (structure)

High Fidelity = Fully dressed product (final UI)
